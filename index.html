Addie

“Bret, I love you. You make every day of my life better, and I feel beyond lucky to have you as my best friend.”

It’s been ten years since I said those words to you in our vows, and without exception, they have been true every day since. It’s not that every day has been perfect (as you know), but the laughter, contemplation, and productive challenge you bring make my days feel full of love and meaning.

I often find myself reflecting on our life and wondering how I won this lottery. Ten years ago, I had high hopes for what the future would hold, but never could I have imagined this. Two sweet, fun-loving kids full of life and personality; a beautiful home in San Francisco with everything we want and need; an active lifestyle full of adventure, delicious food, and new experiences; and, most importantly, it’s all been created through a strong partnership with the most incredible husband.

Bret, you are the smartest person I know. You grow more handsome by the day. You make me cry with your hilarious humor. Your thoughtful questions inspire me daily. And that’s only half of it. You are an extraordinary father. I deeply admire your never-ending patience and energy for our children. Not to mention, your ability to instantly transform into the silliest and most playful kid on the playground. Truly, you are superhuman, and I couldn’t feel luckier to have you on my team.

As I said ten years ago, I promise to be your lifelong partner, and to work every day to be worthy of your commitment. I can’t wait to see what the next decade with you holds.

Happy Birthday and Happy Anniversary

I love you,

Addie

I know we’ve joked about how “perfect” Bret is, and honestly, knowing someone as thoughtful, kind, and genuine as Bret Wallace has raised the bar for me in ways I didn’t expect. Every interaction I’ve had with him—big or small—has left me smiling or laughing or wondering. Even the simplest moments, like when he’d come home from work and we’d talk for ten minutes, or now, quick catch-ups that somehow feel just as meaningful.

One thing I don’t say enough is how much he keeps me going. In a quiet, steady way, he’s helped me feel more confident in who I am, just by being exactly who he is.

One moment that stays with me: in Hawaii, the waves were rough and the water looked gray and intimidating but he still made the effort to go out and show me the fish. He dove under while I stayed at the surface, trying to catch my breath. Like always, he was enthusiastic and encouraging, and somehow gave me the confidence to keep going. I wouldn’t have stopped unless he told me to.

That’s who he is. Someone who shows up, lifts people up, and makes even the uncertain feel a little more possible. I’m really lucky to know him, and even luckier to learn from him.

Happy birthday, Bret.

Rocío xx
